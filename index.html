CHARLES BUKOWSKI(1920–1994)

was an influential German-American poet, novelist, and short-story writer known for his raw, unfiltered depictions of life in Los Angeles. Drawing heavily from his own experiences, Bukowski's works explore themes of love, poverty, addiction, and the human condition, often with a dark sense of humor. His style, characterized by minimalism and honesty, made him a defining figure of the dirty realism and transgressive fiction movements. Notable works include Post Office, Women, and the poetry collection Love Is a Dog from Hell. Bukowski's enduring appeal lies in his gritty portrayal of everyday struggles and his championing of the outsider.

Hollywood by Charles Bukowski is a fictionalized account of the author's experiences making the film Barfly. The book is told from the perspective of Henry "Hank" Chinaski, an alter ego used in other Bukowski novels. The story follows Chinaski as he works with a director, secures and loses financial backing, writes the screenplay, and completes the film.
